# English Grammar

## Present Perfect Tense

Structure: **have / has + past participle**

Used for:
- Past actions with present relevance
- Life experiences (no specific time)
- Actions that started in the past and continue

The {{c1::present perfect}} is formed with {{c2::have/has}} + {{c3::past participle}}.

### Examples

- I **have visited** Japan three times.
- She **has finished** her homework.
- They **have lived** here since 2020.

| Subject | Auxiliary | Past participle |
|---|---|---|
| I / You / We / They | have | seen, gone, eaten… |
| He / She / It | has | seen, gone, eaten… |

## Conditionals

### Type 1: Real conditional (likely)

```
If + present simple, will + base verb
```

If it {{c1::rains}}, I {{c2::will stay}} home.

### Type 2: Unreal conditional (unlikely)

```
If + past simple, would + base verb
```

If I {{c1::had}} a million dollars, I {{c2::would travel}} the world.
